All About Bad Bunny

The Life and Career of Bad Bunny

Bad Bunny, born Benito Antonio Martínez Ocasio in 1994 in Vega Baja, Puerto Rico, is a world-renowned singer, rapper and songwriter. Known as the "King of Latin Trap," he is credited with transforming reggeaton and Latin urban music into a global phenomenon.

Early Life and Career

Bad Bunny grew up in Puerto Rico, where he developed an interest in music while singing in his church choir and uploading songs on SoundCloud. His breakout came in 2016, with the hit "Soy Peor," which gained international attention. Soon after, he signed with Rimas Entertainment, collaborating with artists like J Balvin, Cardi B and Drake.

Bad Bunny's Global Success

Since emerging in 2016, Bad Bunny has released multiple record-breaking albums and has become one of the most streamed artists worldwide. He is known for his bold fashion, unapologetic individuality and blending of Latin trap, reggeaton and other genres. His album "Un Verano Sin Ti" (2022) became the first Spanish-language album nominated for Album of the Year at the Grammys.
